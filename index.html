Search what people actually said this month.

Search results have filled up with pages written for search engines instead of people. RecencyPulse takes your topic straight to recent Reddit threads and TikTok posts — one box, two shortcuts, no wading.

Try it — this box is the real thing

What it does

Time-bounded by default

Reddit searches are pinned to the past month, so you get what people think now — not a thread from 2016 that happens to rank well.

Two clicks, not twelve

No typing "reddit" after every query, no opening a site and hunting for its search box, no fighting with filter menus.

Nothing to set up

Install it, click the icon, type. There's no account, no onboarding, and no settings screen to get lost in.

Keyboard-first

Pick a platform once and press Enter. The extension remembers which one you're aiming at while the popup is open.

What it can't see

  • 01No permissions requested. Check the install screen — Chrome shows nothing to approve, because there's nothing to approve.
  • 02No data collected. Your topics go from the box to the search page. They aren't stored, sent anywhere, or logged.
  • 03No page access. It can't read the sites you visit. It only opens new tabs.
  • 04Source is public. It's about a hundred lines of plain JavaScript. Read all of it in a couple of minutes.
